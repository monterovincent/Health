Ways Men Can Fight & Survive Prostate Cancer

Overview

Prostate cancer is often diagnosed early with appropriate screening. For men with localized disease, the 5‑year survival rate can be near 100% :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}.

Lifestyle changes like diet, exercise, and maintaining healthy weight have been linked to lower risk of progression and mortality :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}.

Lifestyle & Dietary Tips

  • Adopt a plant‑based or Mediterranean style diet: fruits, vegetables, nuts, olive oil, and whole grains :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}.
  • Aim for 150–300 minutes of moderate exercise weekly—cardio and resistance training—as this can lower risk by ~35% :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}.
  • Maintain a healthy BMI and avoid obesity—this helps decrease risk of advanced prostate cancer :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}.
  • Avoid high-fat dairy, red/processed meats, added sugar, and smoking—all linked to elevated risk :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}.

Screening & Medical Care

Regular PSA testing and digital rectal exams help detect cancer early. MRI-based screening is also emerging for better accuracy :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}.

For high-risk or metastatic cases, treatments including hormone therapy, docetaxel chemotherapy, abiraterone, and radiotherapy have improved survival outcomes :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}.
